 V8 Architectural specificationsThe 3-way dipolar, passive loudspeaker shall consist of two 10" low frequency driver with a neodymium magnet assembly, one 8" mid range driver and two 1.4" exit neodymium compression drivers with 2.5" diaphragms mounted to a dedicated wave shaping device and a passive crossover network.The loudspeaker shall only be operated by a dedicated, compatible controller amplifier.The cabinet enclosure shall be made from marine plywood with an impact and weather protected PCP (Polyurea Cabinet Protection) finish.The drivers shall be protected by a rigid metal grill backed by an acoustically transparent foam. The cabinet shall incorporate one handle in each side panel and two additional recessed grips at the rear bottom.The cabinet shall incorporate a three point rigging system for the assembly of vertical line source arrays of up to 24 cabinets in conjunction with a dedicated flying frame.The connection panel on the back shall be recessed and fitted with speakon NLT4 F/M sockets (NL4 M or EP5 connectors as an option).The loudspeaker shall have a nominal horizontal dispersion angle of 80°. The vertical dispersion angle of the assembled array shall be determined by the geometry of the splay angles between the cabinets, which shall be adjustable in a range of 0° to 14° in 1° increments.The power handling capacity shall be 500 W RMS and 2000 W peak (10 ms). The frequency response (–5 dB) measured on axis shall be 67 Hz to 18 kHz with a maximum sound pressure of at least 139 dB.The dimensions (W x H x D) shall not exceed 700 x 310 x 406 mm (27.5" x 12.2" x 18") and shall weigh no more than 34 kg (75 lb).The loudspeaker shall be the V8 by: d&b audiotechnik GmbH & Co. KG.
